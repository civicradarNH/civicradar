## Recording checklist

- Open with the **problem** (Scene 1) before heavy UI chrome — viewers decide in the first 30s.
- **One job per Guidde step** — do not combine report + escalation in one hotspot.
- Say the **disclaimer** once in Scene 2 and again in Scene 7: independent community tool; you file with the corporation.
- In Scene 5, briefly tap all **four hazard types** even if you only submit stagnant water.
- End with Guidde **Call to action** → Play Store URL; label **Install on Google Play**.
- Skip full BMC admin queue / demo logins in this 5-min cut (those belong in longer tours).

### Guidde hotspot click path (manual record)

1. Problem VO over map / title — no click required.
2. Open About (Profile → About) or show home hero.
3. If first-run: city → ward → Join (or skip if already onboarded).
4. Map tab → recenter → pan to show red/green pins.
5. Camera FAB → tap each hazard chip → photo → submit.
6. Tap a neighbour pin → **Me too**.
7. Open escalation / Resources → highlight official channels → show complaint-number field.
8. Community tab → scroll leaderboard / wins / volunteer.
9. Profile → your reports / points.
10. Language switcher EN → HI → EN (or open official-sources.html).
11. Brief “Get involved” / coordinator entry (optional).
12. End card + Guidde CTA → Play Store.

## Continuous voiceover (paste-ready)

Use as a single Guidde voice track if you prefer one take:

> Every monsoon, stagnant water collects in gutters, pits, and blocked drains across Mumbai, Pune, and Thane. Those spots breed mosquitoes — and dengue risk. People know the trouble spots, but filing alone often means no shared map, no status, and either silence or five duplicate complaints. Civic teams don’t get clear, corroborated, geo-tagged signal from the ground.
>
> CivicRadar is a free community ward hazard map for those three cities. It is not a government app and not affiliated with BMC, PMC, or TMC. You pin hazards on a live map, neighbours corroborate with Me too, and when you want an official ticket, you open the corporation’s own channels yourself.
>
> On first open, pick your city and ward — optionally your society. No login for citizens. English, Hindi, Marathi, and Gujarati.
>
> The map shows open pins in red and fixed spots in green. This is the shared picture your lane was missing.
>
> Reporting takes about thirty seconds: choose stagnant water, garbage, a pothole, or a broken streetlight, snap a photo, GPS places the pin, submit. Neighbours see it on the ward map.
>
> Already reported? Tap Me too — collective signal, not duplicate noise.
>
> A pin is not a municipal complaint. When you’re ready, CivicRadar links you to verified channels — BMC 1916 and MyBMC, PMC CARE, TMC, Aaple Sarkar, Swachhata. File there, then save your complaint number in the app and follow escalation until it’s fixed.
>
> On Community, see the ward leaderboard, celebrate wins, volunteer, or pledge supplies. Profile tracks your reports and civic points.
>
> That’s CivicRadar — map it, snap it, report it; rally neighbours; file officially when you choose. Install free from Google Play — CivicRadar, package in.civicradar.app — and put your ward on the map this monsoon.
