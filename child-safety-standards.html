Child Safety Standards

Effective: 3 July 2026 · Last updated: 3 July 2026 · Operator: CivicRadar

Not an official municipal app. CivicRadar is an independent community hazard-mapping tool for Mumbai, Pune, and Thane. It is not operated by any government body.

CivicRadar is committed to preventing child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE), including child sexual abuse material (CSAM), on our platform. This page describes our published standards, moderation practices, and how to report concerns.

1. Intended audience

CivicRadar is intended for users 18 years of age and older. We do not target children and do not knowingly collect personal data from minors. See our Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.

2. Prohibited content and conduct

Users must not upload, share, or attempt to distribute:

  • CSAM or any sexual content involving minors
  • Content that sexualizes, grooms, or exploits children
  • Hazard photos that focus on minors in a non-essential or identifying way
  • Any content that facilitates harm to children

Hazard reports must show the site or hazard (e.g. stagnant water, garbage, potholes, broken streetlights, blocked drains), not people. Do not upload images that clearly identify children.

3. Prevention and moderation

  • On-device checks before upload — file type, size, and basic safety screening
  • EXIF stripping — location and device metadata removed from photos before storage
  • Community-visible map — UGC is limited to hazard reporting; there is no private messaging between users
  • Operator review — coordinators and administrators can remove reports that violate these standards
  • Account and data deletion — users can delete their data in-app (Profile → Delete my data)

4. Reporting CSAM or child safety concerns

If you believe content on CivicRadar violates these standards or involves CSAM:

  1. Email us immediately at civicradarnh@gmail.com with the subject line CivicRadar — Child safety report.
  2. Include the report location (ward/city), date observed, and any relevant details. Do not forward illegal material.
  3. We will review promptly, remove violating content, suspend access where appropriate, and preserve information as required by law.
  4. Where required, we will report to appropriate authorities, including the National Centre for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) or relevant Indian authorities, in accordance with applicable law.

6. Enforcement

Violations may result in immediate removal of content, loss of access to coordinator features, deletion of cloud data tied to the account, and referral to law enforcement. We may update these standards as our app or legal requirements change.
